# Harvest Report: "First Cloud AI Journey"

---

## I. Introduction

The modern technology and operations landscape is evolving rapidly with the powerful growth of Cloud Computing, Artificial Intelligence (AI), and Data Analytics. Through the **"First Cloud AI Journey"** sharing program, I had the opportunity to gain real-world perspectives from experts working at major corporations and companies such as **Colgate-Palmolive**, **Endava Vietnam**, and **Kamereo**.

The session delivered not only in-depth professional knowledge on Data Analytics Engineering and DevOps, but also provided clear guidance on career development pathways and workplace culture within multinational corporations (MNCs).

---

## II. Core Key Takeaways

### 1. A Realistic Understanding of Professional Work

#### Data Analytics Engineer

- The job goes far beyond simply extracting numbers or building static dashboards. The ultimate goal of data analytics is to **solve real business and operational problems** — such as optimizing Last Mile delivery costs, improving Fill Rate order fulfillment, or managing cost risks.
- The two essential core skills are **Critical Thinking** (to identify the Root Cause of problems) and **Data Storytelling** (to transform raw numbers into meaningful insights for decision-makers).

#### DevOps Engineering

- DevOps is not just about memorizing tools (Docker, Kubernetes, AWS) or writing CI/CD scripts, nor is it simply "monitoring systems at midnight." **DevOps is a methodology** aimed at optimizing the software development, testing, and deployment lifecycle.
- The key to success lies in a **solid foundational knowledge base** (Linux, Networking, Python/Golang programming, Automation) combined with **System Thinking** and strong **cross-functional communication skills**.

---

### 2. Self-Assessment via the 5-Level Competency Development Model

The program introduced a clear framework for assessing one's current position and building a personal growth roadmap:

| Level | Title | Characteristics |
|-------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | **Follower** | Masters foundational knowledge and completes guided tasks well |
| 2 | **Learner** | Learns quickly, asks probing questions, and requires mentor guidance |
| 3 | **Problem Solver** | Independently analyzes, proposes solutions, and commits to quality outcomes |
| 4 | **System Thinker** | Views problems in the context of the overall system, anticipates risks, and builds long-term solutions |
| 5 | **Super Star** | Shapes strategic vision and develops the team |

---

### 3. Exposure to Multinational Corporate Culture (MNC Culture)

- **"No-Blame Post-Mortem" Culture:** When incidents or operational risks occur, the organization focuses on identifying root causes from a systems and process perspective, rather than blaming individuals or criticizing colleagues.
- **Hiring and Evaluation Process:** International corporations assess both professional competency (evaluated through the **STAR** model) and **Cultural Fit**, foreign language proficiency, and an **inclusive** mindset that respects diversity.

---

## III. Personal Action Plan

Based on the valuable lessons from the program, I have outlined the following concrete action plan going forward:

- **Fundamentals First:** Prioritize solidifying core knowledge in Linux, Networking, SQL, Python, and application operating principles before chasing new tools or frameworks.
- **Rethinking My Approach:** Break the habit of passively copying code/configs; always ask **"Why"** before asking **"How"** in order to truly understand the essence of every technical problem.
- **Strengthening Soft Skills:** Proactively improve professional English, sharpen critical thinking, and develop the ability to communicate technical information clearly and accessibly.
- **Using AI Smartly:** Leverage AI tools as a powerful assistant to boost productivity and learning efficiency — while never becoming passively dependent on them.
- **Active Community Engagement:** Actively participate in technology communities (such as the AWS Student Builder Group) to gain hands-on experience and expand a professional network.

---

## IV. Conclusion

The most valuable lesson taken from this program is the perspective that: **"Getting hired or landing a new job is only the beginning."** To grow sustainably and demonstrate true value in the era of Cloud Computing and Artificial Intelligence, I recognize my responsibility to **continuously upgrade my skills**, **develop systems thinking**, and **maintain a lifelong learning mindset**.
